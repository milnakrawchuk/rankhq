# Opportunity Framework

The publishing agent should think like a local search strategist, not a content mill.

## What counts as a strong topic

Strong topics usually combine:
- a real city or service area
- a real local service line
- a primary keyword with local or commercial intent
- a buyer pattern that suggests someone may call soon

## Preferred search patterns

Prioritize these patterns:
- `city + service`
- `service + city`
- `emergency + service + city`
- `best + service + city`
- `service + near me`
- `service + cost`
- `service + quote`
- `repair vs replace` style comparison queries
- queries that reveal trust checks: reviews, licensing, response time, financing, same-day availability

## Low-hanging-fruit heuristic

Without a paid keyword tool, define low-hanging fruit as:
- high-intent phrases where current search results are thin, generic, or directory-heavy
- city/service combinations with weak local pages
- questions owners and buyers repeat often during sales calls
- terms that map directly to booked calls, quote requests, or urgent service needs

Do not invent search volume, competition, or conversion data.

## Topic triage

Prefer:
1. specific city + specific service + clear commercial intent
2. service pages, city pages, and comparison pages that support lead generation
3. articles that can internally link into a service cluster

Avoid:
- vague "what is SEO" style topics
- generic advice that could fit any business in any city
- fluff written only to hit a publishing quota
